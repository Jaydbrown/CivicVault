CivicVault Whitepaper

CivicVault: Transparent Community Investment Governance

Version 1.2 · May 2026 · Built on Circle's Arc Network

1. Executive Summary

CivicVault is a community-governance and treasury framework for neighborhood investment groups. It enables communities to form DAOs, fund local projects with USDC stablecoins, vote transparently, and distribute project returns using auditable smart contracts deployed on Circle's Arc — an EVM-compatible L1 where USDC is the native currency.

2. Problem

  • Community investment decisions are often opaque and hard to audit.
  • Manual spreadsheets and chat records create disputes over contributions and payouts.
  • Leadership transitions can break continuity and trust.
  • Members have limited visibility into who approved decisions and when.
  • Stablecoin-based investment groups have no shared infrastructure for governance.

3. Solution

CivicVault provides an on-chain operating model with clear roles and immutable records:

  • A factory contract deploys community DAOs as lightweight EIP-1167 proxy instances.
  • Admins onboard KYC-verified members and create project proposals.
  • Members vote by staking USDC for support, or casting a free downvote.
  • Finance leads propose yield deposits which require 3-of-5 admin multi-sig approval.
  • Members claim yields proportionally to their recorded stake — all on-chain.
  • All transactions settle in USDC with no gas-token price risk.

4. The Arc Network

CivicVault is deployed on Circle's Arc (Chain ID: 5042002), an EVM-compatible L1 where USDC is the native gas currency. Key properties:

  • Every stake, vote weight, and yield payout is denominated in USDC.
  • No wrapped token conversions — the same ERC-20 address is used throughout.
  • Full EVM compatibility means all Solidity tooling (Foundry, Hardhat, Viem) works without changes.
  • Transactions are verifiable at testnet.arcscan.app.

5. Contract System

CivicVaultFactory

  • Deploys a new CivicVault proxy for each community DAO via EIP-1167 minimal clone.
  • Stores and exposes a registry of all deployed DAO addresses and metadata.
  • One shared implementation — gas-efficient onboarding for any number of communities.

CivicVault (per DAO)

  • Role management: creator, admin, finance manager, verified member.
  • Investment lifecycle: create → pending → active → ended/incomplete.
  • Treasury actions: vote stake, multi-sig yield deposit, yield claim, and stake refund.
  • Phase-based escrow: 30% / 40% / 30% milestone release to investment recipients.
  • Activity timeline: every action is logged on-chain with an event and a structured entry.

CivicVaultView (read-only helper)

  • Aggregates DAO and investment data across multiple contracts in a single call.
  • Used by the frontend to minimize RPC round-trips.

7. How Returns Are Shared

This is the core economic mechanism of CivicVault. Every step is enforced by the smart contract — no manual calculation, no off-chain spreadsheet.

Step 1 — Members stake USDC to vote

When a verified member upvotes a proposal they send real USDC to the contract. This stake is locked until the proposal is either activated (funds go into escrow) or marked Incomplete (funds are returned in full). A member's stake is also their proportional claim to any future yield from that investment.

Step 2 — Investment is activated and funds are escrowed

Once the upvote total meets or exceeds the funding target before the deadline, an admin can activate the investment. The contract moves the collected stake into an internal escrow. From this point the funds belong to the investment.

Step 3 — Funds are released in three phases

Admins release capital to the investment recipient in three milestone tranches so that recipients remain accountable:

PhaseAmountTrigger
Phase 130% of escrowed totalAdmin releases after project start
Phase 240% of escrowed totalAdmin releases at mid-point milestone
Phase 3Remaining 30%Admin releases on completion

Step 4 — Finance manager proposes a yield deposit

When the investment generates a return (rent, loan repayment, revenue share, etc.), a finance manager calls proposeYieldDeposit(investmentId, amount, expenseReportCID). This creates a pending proposal on-chain with a reference to the expense report pinned to IPFS.

Step 5 — Admins approve with multi-sig (3-of-5)

At least 3 out of 5 admins must each call approveYieldDeposit(proposalId) before the yield can be deposited. This prevents a single actor from unilaterally moving money. Each approval is recorded on-chain and emits an event.

Step 6 — Yield is deposited into the contract

Once 3 approvals are recorded, the proposer (or any admin / creator) calls executeYieldDeposit(proposalId). The contract pulls the USDC from the finance manager's wallet into the contract and credits the investment's yield balance.

Step 7 — Members claim their proportional share

Each upvoting member calls claimYield(investmentId). The contract calculates their share using the formula:

memberYield = (memberStake / totalUpvoteStake) × totalYieldDeposited

For example: if the total upvote stake was 10,000 USDC and a member staked 1,000 USDC (10%), and the total yield deposited was 2,000 USDC, that member receives 200 USDC.

Every payout is proportional to contribution. Members who stake more receive a larger share. Members who downvoted receive no yield. Unclaimed yield is held on-chain for a 90-day grace period before admins may recover it to the DAO treasury.

Grace period and unclaimed yield

After an investment is closed, members have 90 days to claim their yield. After the grace period expires, an admin can call sweepUnclaimedYield(investmentId, recipient) to recover the remaining balance to any address (typically the DAO treasury). This prevents funds from being permanently stranded on-chain.

9. Current Product (Implemented)

  • Landing experience: trust, features, governance overview, and investment categories.
  • Dashboard: wallet-connected portfolio, metric cards, activity feed, and role indicators.
  • DAO discovery and detail views with investment, member, and timeline tabs.

10. Risks

  • Smart contract vulnerabilities and integration defects — mitigated by Foundry test suite.
  • Role-key compromise and operational misuse — mitigated by multi-sig yield controls.
  • Off-chain project execution risk — CivicVault governs treasury, not physical delivery.
  • Regulatory and legal compliance — each DAO community is responsible for local compliance.

11. Roadmap

  • Mainnet deployment on Arc after security audit.
  • Configurable grace periods and quorum thresholds per DAO.
  • Batch member onboarding and KYC hash update flows.
  • Investment document update and category-based discovery.
  • DAO reputation and participation scoring.

12. Conclusion

CivicVault turns informal neighborhood investment coordination into a transparent, rules-driven system. By combining on-chain role governance, USDC staking, multi-sig yield approval, and proportional payout logic, it makes every contribution, decision, and payout verifiable — for every member, at any time.
